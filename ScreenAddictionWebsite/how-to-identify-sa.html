HOW TO IDENTIFY SCREEN ADDICTION   

Now that technology has become a part of our everyday lives, screen addiction is extremely common especially in children. According to a 2019 study done by Common Sense Media, kids aged 8-12 spend   around 5 hours in front of a screen daily with teens clocking in at around 7 not including at school or for homework. (pg. 5) If you aren't sure if you or your child are struggling with screen addiction, here are     ways to help identify it.                           

  1. Spending less time with family and friends and declining social invitations. 
  2. Frequent conflict with friends and family about screen time.
  3.  Lying about how much time is spent gaming or on electronic devices.
  4.  Blaming other people for problems caused by spending too much time in front of a screen.
  5.  Denying that there is a problem.
  6. Breaking promises to stop excessive use of a device.
