how to break screen addiction

There are both pros and cons to the access of technology. Technology can help us with everday tasks such as making a grocery list, doing calculations, taking pictures or using a gps. Unfortunately even with the opportunities that have come with the digital age there are also quite a few draw backs. Screen addiction is one of the major ways that technology can affect our lives. Many people may struggle with screen addiction but may not know it. If you think you or your child may be struggling with screen addiction here are some ways to help you cope.           

For children:

  • According to the World Health Organization children should spend no more than 1 hour a day in front of a screen. (pg.8 p.3)
  •  Make sure your child is using technology in ways that benefit them creatively rather then ways that waste time. 
  •  Start setting limits early so that when they get older they can learn how to balance their time between sitting in front of a screen and properly stimulating their brain.    
  •  Teach them to foster real-life friendships.
  •  Set a good example for them.

For adults:

  • Turn off all notifications.
  • Go grayscale.
  •  Take social media off your phone.
  •  Charge your devices outside of your bedroom.
  •  Limit what your see on your homescreen.
